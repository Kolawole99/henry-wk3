# Corporate Travel Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024
**Booking Portal:** CompanyTravel (Navan/Egencia)
**After-Hours Support:** +1-800-555-0199

## Booking Guidelines
All business travel must be booked through the **Company Travel Portal**. Booking outside the portal (e.g., directly on Expedia or Kayak) is prohibited unless there is a specific technical outage.

### Advance Booking
* **Flights:** Must be booked at least **14 days in advance**.
* **Approvals:** Trips booked less than 7 days in advance require VP-level approval.

## Air Travel
* **Domestic Flights (< 6 hours):** Economy / Main Cabin class.
* **International/Long-Haul (> 6 hours):** Premium Economy is permitted. Business Class requires EVP approval.
* **Airline Selection:** Travelers must select the **Lowest Logical Fare** (flights within a 2-hour window of requested time).
* **Upgrades:** Personal miles may be used to upgrade, but the company will not reimburse the cost of an upgrade.

## Lodging (Hotels)
The company reimburses standard room rates. Suites are not permitted.

### City Tier Limits (Nightly Rate before tax)
* **Tier 1 (NYC, SF, London, Tokyo):** Up to **$350/night**.
* **Tier 2 (Chicago, Austin, Boston, Paris):** Up to **$275/night**.
* **Tier 3 (Standard):** Up to **$200/night**.

If a conference hotel exceeds these rates, book the conference rate and note "Conference Hotel" in the expense description.

## Ground Transportation
* **Rideshare (Uber/Lyft):** Standard or X level only. "Black" or "Lux" cars are not reimbursed unless sharing with 3+ colleagues creates cost parity.
* **Rental Cars:** Permitted for trips involving substantial driving (>50 miles/day).
    * **Class:** Intermediate/Mid-size.
    * **Insurance:** **Decline** the rental agency insurance (CDW/LDW). The company holds a blanket liability policy for business rentals.
* **Personal Car:** Reimbursed at the current IRS mileage rate (currently **67 cents per mile** for 2024). Gas is not reimbursed separately if claiming mileage.

## Bleisure (Business + Leisure)
Employees may extend a business trip for personal vacation ("Bleisure") under these conditions:
1.  **Cost Neutral:** The flight cost must not exceed the cost of the business-only itinerary. If the extension increases the fare, the employee pays the difference.
2.  **Expenses:** All meals, lodging, and transport on personal days are 100% the employee's responsibility.
3.  **PTO:** Vacation days must be logged in the HRIS system.

## Detailed Booking Procedures

### Accessing the Travel Portal
1.  Navigate to **https://travel.company.com**
2.  Log in using your SSO credentials (same as email)
3.  Complete travel profile on first use:
    - Frequent flyer numbers
    - Hotel loyalty programs
    - TSA PreCheck/Global Entry number
    - Seating preferences
    - Dietary restrictions

### Booking Flights - Step by Step
1.  Click **"Book Flight"** from dashboard
2.  Enter origin, destination, and travel dates
3.  Select flight from results:
    - System highlights "Lowest Logical Fare" in green
    - Flights within 2 hours of request time, within $100 of cheapest
4.  Review fare class:
    - Basic Economy NOT allowed (no seat selection, no changes)
    - Main/Standard Economy required minimum
5.  Add travelers (if booking for multiple people)
6.  Review total cost and submit for approval
7.  Receive approval email (typically within 2 hours for standard trips)
8.  Return to portal to complete booking
9.  E-ticket confirmation sent to your email

**Tip:** Book Tuesday-Thursday for best rates. Avoid booking on Fridays/Sundays.

### Booking Hotels - Step by Step
1.  In travel portal, click **"Book Hotel"**
2.  Enter destination and dates
3.  Filter by nightly rate limit based on city tier
4.  Consider:
    - Proximity to meeting location/office
    - Included amenities (Wi-Fi, breakfast)
    - Company preferred hotels (Marriott, Hilton, Hyatt) for points
5.  Review hotel details and cancellation policy
6.  Select room type: Standard room only (no suites)
7.  Submit booking (hotels typically don't require pre-approval if under rate limit)
8.  Confirmation sent to email with hotel address and reservation code

**Conference Hotels:** If conference hotel exceeds tier limit, still book there for convenience. Note "Conference Hotel" in expense report.

### Booking Ground Transportation
**Rental Cars:**
1.  In travel portal, click **"Book Rental Car"**
2.  Enter location and dates
3.  Select Intermediate or Mid-size category
4.  Choose company preferred vendors: Enterprise, National, Hertz
5.  **Important:** DECLINE all insurance (CDW/LDW/LIS)
    - Company has blanket business auto policy
    - Accepting insurance wastes company money
6.  Add additional drivers if traveling with colleagues
7.  Confirm booking

**Rideshare/Taxi:**
- Book directly through Uber/Lyft apps
- Use company credit card or submit for reimbursement
- Standard/UberX level only
- Save receipt for expense report

### Travel for Multiple People
If booking for team members:
1.  Use "Multi-Traveler" booking option
2.  Enter each traveler's name and employee ID
3.  System splits cost across respective department budgets
4.  Each traveler receives own confirmation
5.  Manager of most senior person approves overall trip

### Changing or Cancelling Travel

**Changes:**
1.  Log into travel portal -> "My Trips"
2.  Select trip and click "Modify"
3.  Make changes (fees may apply depending on fare rules)
4.  If change increases cost >$100, requires manager re-approval
5.  System shows change fees upfront before confirming

**Cancellations:**
1.  Cancel as early as possible to minimize fees
2.  "My Trips" -> Select trip -> "Cancel"
3.  Review refund/credit policy:
    - Most flights: Credit for future travel (minus change fee)
    - Hotels: Free cancellation if >24-48 hours in advance
    - Rental cars: Free cancellation typically
4.  Confirm cancellation
5.  Manager and finance automatically notified

**Emergency Changes (same-day):**
- Call after-hours travel support: +1-800-555-0199
- They can rebook in real-time for flight disruptions
- Company policy covers reasonable same-day changes

## Meals & Per Diem

### Meals While Traveling
Employees are reimbursed for actual meal costs (not flat per diem):
- **Breakfast:** Up to $25
- **Lunch:** Up to $30
- **Dinner:** Up to $75
- **Daily maximum:** $130

**Receipt Requirements:**
- Meals >$25 require itemized receipt
- Credit card slip alone not sufficient
- Must show what was ordered

**What's NOT Reimbursable:**
- Meals on travel days when in the air during meal time (airline provides food/snacks)
- Alcohol as primary expense (e.g., bar tab without food)
- Room service (go to restaurant unless extenuating circumstance)
- Minibar charges

### Business Meals with Clients
- Up to $100 per person (including tax and gratuity)
- Must list all attendees in expense report
- Must note business purpose ("Discussed Q4 partnership terms")
- Alcohol permitted in moderation (1-2 drinks with meal)

### Team Dinners
- Once per quarter per team
- $50 per person maximum
- Requires VP approval if more frequent
- Great for morale but must be business-related (project kickoff, team building)

## International Travel

### Pre-Travel Requirements
For all international business travel:
1.  **Passport:** Must be valid 6+ months beyond return date
2.  **Visa:** Check requirements at travel.state.gov
    - Company will reimburse visa fees
    - Apply 6-8 weeks in advance
3.  **Travel Insurance:** Automatically included for international trips
4.  **Security Briefing:** Required for travel to high-risk countries
    - Contact security@company.com
5.  **Vaccinations:** Confirm requirements and get shots
    - Reimbursable through health FSA or expense report

### International Flight Booking
- **Flights >6 hours:** Premium Economy allowed
- **Flights >10 hours:** Business Class with EVP approval
- **Red-eye flights:** Encouraged to minimize days out of office
- **Airport lounges:** Allowed for international flights >4 hour layover (submit receipt)

### International Hotels
Rate limits adjusted by location:
- **Tier 1 (London, Tokyo, Zurich):** $400/night
- **Tier 2 (European capitals, major Asian cities):** $300/night
- **Tier 3 (Other international):** $250/night

### Currency & Expenses
- Expenses in foreign currency: Convert to USD for expense report
- Use XE.com or credit card statement conversion rate
- Keep foreign receipts (even if not in English) - attach to expense report
- Company credit cards automatically convert at point of sale

### International Per Diem
Meal limits adjusted for high cost-of-living cities:
- **Tier 1 (Zurich, Tokyo, London):** $200/day total
- **Tier 2 (Most European/Asian capitals):** $150/day total
- **Tier 3 (Other locations):** $130/day (standard)

Check city tier list at: **travel.company.com/international-rates**

## Travel Safety & Security

### Travel Registry
For international travel and domestic travel to high-risk areas:
1.  Register trip at **travel.company.com/registry**
2.  Provide: destination, dates, hotel, emergency contact
3.  Security team monitors registered trips
4.  Receive safety alerts and updates

### Emergency Contacts While Traveling
- **Travel Support 24/7:** +1-800-555-0199
- **Security Emergency:** +1-800-555-0155
- **Medical Emergency:** Call local emergency services first, then notify security team

### Travel Health
- **Travel Insurance:** Covers medical emergencies abroad (auto-enrolled)
- **Prescription Medications:** Carry in original bottles, bring extra supply
- **Medical Kit:** Consider small first aid kit for international trips
- **Notify Manager:** If you fall ill during business travel

### Personal Safety Tips
1.  Don't display company laptop/materials in public
2.  Use hotel safe for passport and valuables
3.  Be aware of surroundings, especially in unfamiliar cities
4.  Avoid discussing sensitive business topics in public
5.  Don't use public/hotel Wi-Fi for work - use VPN

## Special Circumstances

### Traveling with Family
- **Policy:** Personal travel mixed with business is allowed (see Bleisure)
- **Flight:** Only your seat reimbursed
- **Hotel:** Company pays for standard room rate (no suite upgrade)
- **Meals:** Only your meals reimbursed, not family members
- **Rental Car:** Company pays full car rental if needed for business

### Traveling with Disabilities or Medical Needs
- **Seating:** Premium seat upgrade approved for medical necessity (doctor's note required)
- **Assistance:** Request special assistance through travel portal
- **Service Animals:** Automatically accommodated
- **Medical Equipment:** Coordinate with travel support for proper handling

### Traveling While Pregnant
- **Doctor Clearance:** Required for air travel after 36 weeks
- **Seating:** Extra legroom seats allowed (will not count as upgrade)
- **Comfort Considerations:** Manager may approve premium economy without standard approval
- **Flexibility:** Trip cancellation policies more lenient

### Last-Minute Emergency Travel
- **Family Emergency:** Coordinate with manager and HR
- **Company may assist with booking/payment**
- **Keep receipts for potential reimbursement**
- **Take time you need, travel policy flexible in emergencies**

## Troubleshooting Travel Issues

### Issue 1: Flight Delayed/Cancelled
**Solution:**
1.  Contact airline immediately for rebooking options
2.  If significant delay (>3 hours), call travel support: +1-800-555-0199
3.  Expenses for meal during delay are reimbursable
4.  If overnight stay required: Company covers hotel
5.  Keep all receipts and document situation in expense report

### Issue 2: Hotel Overbooked
**Solution:**
1.  Ask hotel to arrange alternative accommodation of equal/better quality
2.  If alternative is more expensive, hotel typically covers difference
3.  If you must pay difference, submit for reimbursement with explanation
4.  Call travel support if hotel is uncooperative

### Issue 3: Lost/Delayed Luggage
**Solution:**
1.  File claim with airline immediately (keep claim number)
2.  Purchase essential items (toiletries, change of clothes): Up to $150 reimbursable
3.  Save receipts for reasonable interim purchases
4.  Submit expense report with explanation and claim number

### Issue 4: Rental Car Issues
**Solution:**
1.  If car breaks down: Call rental company for replacement
2.  If accident: File police report, call rental company, notify company security team
3.  Do NOT accept blame or sign anything beyond standard accident report
4.  Company insurance will handle claims

### Issue 5: Travel Portal Won't Accept Booking
**Solution:**
1.  Check that dates are at least 14 days in advance (7 days requires VP approval)
2.  Verify your profile is complete
3.  Ensure you're selecting compliant fare class
4.  If technical issue: Call travel support or email travel-help@company.com

### Issue 6: Exceeded Nightly Hotel Rate Limit
**Solution:**
1.  Check if city has special tier rate (conference city, high-cost city)
2.  If conference hotel: Book anyway, note "Conference Hotel" in expense
3.  If truly no options under limit: Book best available, explain in expense report
4.  Manager can approve overage with justification

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q1: Can I earn airline/hotel points on company travel?**
A: Yes! You keep all frequent flyer miles and hotel points earned on business travel.

**Q2: Can I use a personal credit card instead of corporate card?**
A: Yes, but you must submit for reimbursement. Corporate card is preferred for easier expense tracking.

**Q3: What if I need to travel with less than 14 days notice?**
A: Requires VP-level approval. Mark as urgent in travel portal and explain business need.

**Q4: Can I fly into a different city for personal reasons?**
A: Only if flight cost is same or less than direct routing to business destination. You pay any difference.

**Q5: What if I want to stay over a Saturday night to save on airfare?**
A: Allowed if total cost (flight + extra hotel night) is less than non-Saturday flight. Must document savings.

**Q6: Are meals on the airplane reimbursable?**
A: No, airline-provided food is included in ticket. Airport meals before flight are reimbursable (within limits).

**Q7: Can I book Airbnb instead of a hotel?**
A: Generally no, unless hotel options are limited. Must be business location (not residential). Requires manager approval.

**Q8: What about TSA PreCheck or Global Entry fees?**
A: Reimbursable if you travel 4+ times per year. Submit receipt with next expense report.

**Q9: Can I upgrade to Business Class with my own money?**
A: Yes, using personal funds or miles is allowed. Company won't reimburse but doesn't prohibit.

**Q10: What if my manager wants me to stay at a specific hotel over limit?**
A: Manager can approve overage. They should note approval in your travel request or expense report.

**Q11: Do I need approval for every trip?**
A: Yes, all trips must be booked through portal with manager approval (automatic for routine trips).

**Q12: What constitutes a business trip vs. commuting?**
A: Business trip: Travel to location other than your regular office. Commuting: Travel to your assigned primary workplace (never reimbursable).

**Q13: Can I combine multiple client visits in one trip?**
A: Yes, encouraged! More efficient. Book multi-city itinerary through travel portal.

**Q14: What if I'm traveling to the same city as a colleague?**
A: Coordinate travel to potentially share rental car. You can each expense your portion.

**Q15: Is travel insurance really necessary?**
A: Yes, especially internationally. Company provides this automatically for all business travel at no cost to you.

## Travel Expense Reporting

### Submitting Travel Expenses
1.  Complete trip and collect all receipts
2.  Within 30 days, log into expense portal: **expenses.company.com**
3.  Create expense report (name it with trip purpose: "Seattle Client Visit - Jan 2024")
4.  Add expenses:
    - Flights (usually auto-imported from corporate card)
    - Hotels (attach itemized folio, not just credit card slip)
    - Meals (itemized receipts for >$25)
    - Ground transportation (Uber receipts, rental car receipts, mileage log)
5.  Attach receipts to each expense line item
6.  Submit for manager approval
7.  Reimbursement via direct deposit within 5-7 business days

### What Receipts to Save
**Required:**
- Flight confirmation (e-ticket)
- Hotel folio (itemized bill showing room rate, taxes, dates)
- Rental car contract and final receipt
- Meals >$25 (itemized showing food/drinks ordered)
- Any client entertainment receipts

**Not Required:**
- Boarding passes (TSA requirement only)
- Uber/taxi receipts <$25 (though good practice to save)
- Meals <$25 when traveling alone
