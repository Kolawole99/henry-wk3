## FAQ
**Q: What is the HSA contribution limit for 2024?**
A: $4,150 for individuals and $8,300 for families (ages 55+ can contribute an additional $1,000 catch-up). The company contributes $500/year to your HSA if you choose the HDHP, deposited in two installments (January and July).

**Q: Do I get life insurance?**
A: Yes, we provide Basic Life AD&D equal to **1x your annual salary** at no cost to you. You may purchase up to 5x salary in supplemental coverage through voluntary enrollment.

**Q: Can I cover my domestic partner?**
A: Yes, domestic partners are eligible for health, dental, and vision coverage. You must complete an Affidavit of Domestic Partnership and provide proof of shared financial responsibility (joint lease, bank account, or mortgage).

**Q: What happens to my HSA if I leave the company?**
A: Your HSA is portable and belongs to you. All funds remain yours, including the company contribution. You can continue using it for qualified medical expenses or roll it over to a new HSA at your next employer.

**Q: Are there prescription drug benefits?**
A: Yes, both health plans include prescription coverage through CVS Caremark. Tier 1 (generic) drugs have a $10 copay, Tier 2 (preferred brand) $40, and Tier 3 (non-preferred brand) $75. Mail-order pharmacy available for 90-day supplies at 2.5x the copay.

## Appendix: Glossary of Benefits Terms

**Deductible:** Amount you pay out-of-pocket before insurance coverage kicks in.

**Coinsurance:** Percentage of costs you pay after meeting your deductible (e.g., 90% coinsurance = you pay 10%).

**Out-of-Pocket Maximum:** The most you'll pay in a year; after this, insurance covers 100%.

**Premium:** Monthly cost to maintain insurance coverage.

**HSA (Health Savings Account):** Tax-advantaged savings account for medical expenses; funds roll over year to year.

**FSA (Flexible Spending Account):** Pre-tax account for medical or dependent care expenses; use-it-or-lose-it annually.

**COBRA:** Temporary continuation of health coverage after leaving the company (employee pays full premium + 2% admin fee).

**Vesting:** The process by which you gain ownership of employer contributions (e.g., 401(k) match).

**Safe Harbor 401(k):** Employer match that is 100% vested immediately.

**Qualifying Life Event (QLE):** Life change allowing mid-year benefit enrollment changes (marriage, birth, loss of coverage, etc.).
